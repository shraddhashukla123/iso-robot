This page is the "so what." It explains, in business terms, why ISO Robot exists
and what measurably improves when an organisation adopts it.

## The core idea

> Every enterprise risk should be **traceable to evidence** and **scored by a method
> you can defend** — not by the instinct of whoever happened to run the assessment.

ISO Robot enforces exactly that. The result is a risk register that survives
scrutiny from auditors, regulators, and the board.

## What changes for each stakeholder

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Risk & compliance lead">
    - Stop rebuilding the register from scratch each cycle — re-run the pipeline
      on the latest documents.
    - Defend every rating with a transparent method and a source-page trail.
    - Spot coverage gaps: which requirements produced no risks, which risks have
      weak controls.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Analyst">
    - Skip the manual reading and copy-paste. Review and refine instead of transcribe.
    - Consistent vocabulary and scoring across every issue you touch.
    - Focus your judgment where it matters — selecting and contextualising risks.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Executive / board">
    - A prioritised, rated view of enterprise risk in business language.
    - Confidence that numbers are repeatable and evidence-backed.
    - A faster path from "new regulation published" to "risk position understood."
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Before and after

| Dimension | Manual process | With ISO Robot |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Document review | Days–weeks of reading | Automated extraction with page references |
| Rating consistency | Varies by analyst | Fixed matrices — identical inputs, identical output |
| Traceability | Hard to reconstruct | Built in: risk → issue → requirement → page |
| Repeatability | Re-done each cycle | Re-run on updated documents |
| Auditability | Manual evidence gathering | Evidence is the data model |

## The principle that makes it defensible

ISO Robot deliberately separates **judgment** from **calculation**:

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="AI provides judgment" icon="robot">
    Reading the documents and forming qualitative opinions — how likely, how
    severe, how effective the existing controls are.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fixed rules do the maths" icon="calculator">
    Inherent risk, residual risk, and the recommended response are computed by
    published matrices — not invented by the model.
  </Card>
</Columns>

<Check>
Because the rating maths is a transparent matrix rather than an AI output, the same
judgments **always** produce the same rating — and the policy can be tuned to your
risk appetite without re-training or re-prompting anything.
</Check>

## Where the AI helps — and where it doesn't

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What the AI does" icon="circle-check">
    Reads dense regulatory text, extracts discrete requirements, clusters them into
    risk themes, classifies them across analytical lenses, and proposes qualitative
    risk judgments.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What the AI never does" icon="circle-xmark">
    Decide a final risk rating, the inherent/residual level, or the recommended
    response. Those are computed deterministically so they are auditable and tunable.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens if the AI is unavailable" icon="shield-halved">
    The pipeline falls back to deterministic heuristics where one exists, so the
    chain still produces usable output instead of failing outright.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Designed for the real world

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Multi-tenant by default" icon="building-lock">

    from another's.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Resilient to messy inputs" icon="file-shield">
    Oversized PDFs, scanned layouts, and partial failures are handled gracefully —
    one bad page never stops the run.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Responsive at any scale" icon="bolt">
    Heavy work runs as background jobs, so the system stays usable while large

  </Card>
  <Card title="Tunable to your appetite" icon="sliders">
    The scoring matrices are policy, not code — adjust them to match how your
    organisation defines risk.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
